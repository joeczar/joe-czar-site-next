## Humanitarian Work - Save the Children Deutschland

### Tempelhof Child Friendly Spaces (Dec 2015 - Mar 2017)

**Context:**
- ~2,000 refugees (40% children) housed in former Tempelhof Airport hangars
- First implementation of the Child Friendly Spaces (CFS) concept in Germany
- Funded by IKEA Deutschland, IKEA Stiftung, C&A, C&A Foundation, The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

**Joe's Role:**
- Helped establish the Child Friendly Spaces infrastructure
- **Started the internet/computer workshops** (his initiative)
- Designed and implemented computer labs for kids and caregivers
- Scaled program to **Raspberry Pi + Chromebook labs in 5 refugee shelters**

**Activities at CFS:**
- Free play, singing, crafts, dancing
- German language classes
- Psychosocial support
- Digital literacy workshops

**Staff included:**
- Language teachers
- Psychologist
- Theater pedagogue
- Intercultural pedagogue
- Child rights expert
- Trained volunteers

### MakerSpace Project (Joe's Design)

Joe designed the MakerSpace concept, later implemented 2019-2021 in Berlin schools.

**Equipment:**
- Lasercutter (cutting/engraving prototypes)
- 3D printer (physical models)
- Soldering station (electronics basics)
- Calliope microcontrollers (programming education)
- Greenscreen (video production)
- Recording studio (audio production)

**Focus:**
- Children disadvantaged in education system
- Non-German speaking households
- Children in poverty
- Digital literacy and children's rights online
- Creativity, problem-solving, collaboration

**Partners:**
Adidas, Capgemini, GSK Deutschland, tesa, Wacker Neuson

**References:**
- https://www.savethechildren.de/informieren/einsatzorte/deutschland/bildung/makerspace/
- https://www.savethechildren.de/informieren/einsatzorte/deutschland/migration-und-flucht/schutz-und-spielraeume/
